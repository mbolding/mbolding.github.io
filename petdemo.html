Positron Emission Tomography (PET)

PET imaging relies on the annihilation of positrons and electrons. When a positron is emitted from a radiotracer, it travels a short distance (positron range) before encountering an electron. The resulting annihilation produces two 511 keV photons traveling in approximately opposite directions.
