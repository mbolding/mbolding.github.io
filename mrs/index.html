General Considerations at 3T

  • Hardware & Instrumentation Requirements for non-proton imaging on clinical scanners.
    • Broadband amplifiers (Multi-transmit capable?)
    • Double-tuned coils (e.g., 1H/31P, 1H/13C) for anatomical localization + shimming
    • B0 Shimming challenges (2nd order shims essential)
  • Pulse Sequences & Physics Adapting standard sequences for X-nuclei.
    • FID vs. Spin Echo (T2* considerations)
    • NOE (Nuclear Overhauser Effect) enhancement - proton decoupling
    • SAR (Specific Absorption Rate) limits with decoupling
    • Chemical Shift Imaging (CSI) / MRSI vs. SVS (Single Voxel)

31P Phosphorus Spectroscopy

  • Bioenergetics The primary clinical application of 31P MRS.
    • PCr (Phosphocreatine) / ATP ratios - Energy reserve status
    • Pi (Inorganic Phosphate) - Metabolic byproducts
    • Intracellular pH measurement (chemical shift of Pi)
    • Mg2+ concentration (chemical shift difference between ATP resonances)
  • Membrane Metabolism Markers of synthesis and degradation.
    • PME (Phosphomonoesters) - Membrane building blocks (e.g., PC, PE)
    • PDE (Phosphodiesters) - Membrane breakdown products (e.g., GPC, GPE)
    • Relevance in oncology (turnover rates)

13C Carbon Spectroscopy

  • Metabolic Flux Analysis Tracking dynamic metabolic pathways.
    • Natural Abundance (1.1%) challenges - requires long scan times. See: Liver Glycogen
    • 13C-Glucose infusion studies (TCA cycle rate, Glutamate/Glutamine cycling)
    • Glycogen metabolism (Muscle/Liver)
  • Hyperpolarized 13C (Pyruvate) Emerging clinical applications (requires polarizer).
    • Real-time metabolic imaging (Pyruvate -> Lactate conversion)
    • Warburg effect visualization in tumors

129Xe Xenon Spectroscopy

  • Hyperpolarized Imaging Using laser-polarized gas for enhanced signal.
    • Lung ventilation imaging (Gas phase)
    • Dissolved Phase Spectroscopy (The "MRS" component)
  • Dissolved Phase Peaks Chemical shift separation of compartments.
    • Gas peak (0 ppm reference)
    • Barrier peak (Tissue/Plasma ~197 ppm)
    • RBC peak (Red Blood Cells ~217 ppm)
    • Gas exchange metrics (RBC/Barrier ratios) - Pulmonary fibrosis, COPD assessment
