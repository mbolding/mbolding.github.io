Request for Applications

Neuroengineering Pilot Award Program

UAB Consortium for Neuroengineering and Brain-Computer Interfaces (CNBCI)

Supporting high-risk, early-stage ideas in neural circuit analysis and neuroengineering.

Program Overview

The goal of this program is to provide funding to faculty who study the function of neural circuits using techniques such as neurophysiology, neuromodulation, in vivo optogenetics, fiber photometry, miniscopes, and other cutting-edge approaches. The funding will support early-stage research that has not yet received federal funding (R01 or equivalent).

Eligibility

Applications are welcome from UAB faculty members who are actively involved in research related to in vivo neural circuit analysis or neuroengineering topics. Specifically, this RFA is open to:

  • Assistant Professors
  • Early Associate Professors (time in rank < 2 years)
  • Applicants must not have active major funding (e.g., R01 or equivalent).

Application Requirements

Applications should include the following components submitted as a single document:

  • A one-page research proposal in the format of an NIH Specific Aims page
  • The PI's NIH-style biosketch
  • A brief budget and budget justification
  • Email subject line: CNBCI pilot award 2026
