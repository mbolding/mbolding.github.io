Chapter 3: Vector Calculus

A vector-valued function $\mathbf{r}(t) = \langle x(t), y(t) \rangle$ describes the path of a particle in the plane. Velocity $\mathbf{v}(t) = \mathbf{r}'(t)$ is tangent to the path.
Acceleration $\mathbf{a}(t) = \mathbf{r}''(t)$ points toward the "turn". By observing the velocity and acceleration vectors in motion, we gain intuition about how forces shape trajectories.
